# Interview Summary: In-Home Daycare Provider
**Interviewee**: Lara (speaker_2)  
**Interviewer**: Zach (speaker_0)  
**Date**: 2026-02-18
**Duration**: ~45 minutes

## Executive Summary
Lara is an in-home daycare provider who has been licensed for approximately one year. She currently uses KinderSmart for attendance tracking (mandated by her licensor) and KidCare for the food program. The interview reveals significant pain points around paperwork management, onboarding processes, and attendance tracking technology issues.

## Top Priority Pain Points

### 1. Onboarding New Children
**Current State**:
- 30-page handbooks per child that need to be printed
- Parents often don't have printers at home, requiring provider to print everything
- Only prints signature pages and emails the rest to save paper
- Managing signed documents is messy and disorganized
- Large pile of paperwork that's difficult to sort and track

**Impact**: 
- Most time-consuming process
- Creates ongoing filing and organization problems
- Frustration with paper management

**Ideal Solution**:
> "What I had envisioned in my head is like, there's an app... all the paperwork comes over, right? And they have the option to, like, e-sign or print, or if they do print, then scan back in, it automatically uploads that into it, and then it just comes straight back to me."

### 2. Receipt & Paperwork Management
**Current State**:
- Big box of receipts for tax deductions
- Receipts frequently get lost (toddler steals them)
- Missing receipts from last year
- Multiple piles of mail and paperwork around printer and desk
- Invoices from DCYF (even though she does them electronically)
- Licensing documents, certificates, and general paperwork is disorganized
- First year filing as a business - uncertain about tax process

**Impact**: 
- Risk of losing tax deductions on expensive purchases ($400+ receipts)
- Creates mess and clutter
- Stress about tax compliance
- Time spent organizing and searching for documents

**Ideal Solution**:
> "It'd be so nice if I could just automatically, like, scan the receipts to my phone. And then, like, just have them there, and then have them, like, printed when I need... I could just be in WinCo and be like, scan, okay, it's stored. Lila, here's the receipt."

### 3. Attendance Tracking System (KinderSmart)
**Current State**:
- Mandated by state licensor (no choice in software)
- Doesn't work on phones
- Bought new tablet hoping to enable parent self-check-in
- Tablet is "too new" - incompatible with the software
- Customer service said they've been working on the issues for a month, advised to check back in 2-3 weeks
- System hasn't worked since November (currently working through this interview timeframe)
- Parents can't do their own attendance
- Manual logging required by provider

**Impact**: 
- State compliance requirement
- Cannot empower parents to sign in/out themselves
- Time wasted trying to find compatible technology
- Ongoing frustration with outdated system

**Purpose of Attendance**:
- Required by state licensing
- Licensor checks for parent-child ratio compliance
- Protection against disputes (e.g., estranged parents)
- Critical for subsidy kids - state monitors closely to prevent fraud

## Secondary Pain Points

### 4. Parent Communication & Photo Sharing
**Current State**:
- Sends individual pictures to each parent
- Has group photos but can't share them due to privacy concerns
- Some parents don't authorize their children to be in shared photos
- No way to do group communication without sharing phone numbers

**Ideal Solution**:
- Group chat functionality where parents can't see each other's contact info
- Photo sharing with terms that photos can't be taken from the app and posted elsewhere
- Referenced Champions program app model that had these features

### 5. Food Program Logging
**Current State**:
- Uses KidCare website (through CACFP sponsor program)
- Daily log during nap time: mark attendance, enter food served (breakfast, lunch, etc.), assign to each child
- Must be logged before midnight or lose reimbursement for that day
- Monthly submission to sponsor

**Overall Assessment**: System works relatively smoothly

**Issue**: Midnight deadline creates pressure - solution is to do it during nap time consistently

### 6. Getting Licensed (Past Issue, Future Provider Concern)
**Experience**:
- Year-long licensing process with no clear guidance
- Multiple inspections (fire, health, septic/water depending on property)
- Numerous specific requirements (outlet types, emergency windows, anchoring furniture)
- Licensor very specific on some things, flexible on others (inconsistent)
- Different people giving different requirements
- Had to navigate entire process alone before getting an early learning coach

**Coach Access**: Only available AFTER becoming licensed and enrolled in early learning

**Ideal Solution**:
- Step-by-step guided process
- Checklist based on specific property situation (well water vs. city, septic vs. sewer)
- Required supplies list
- Required education list
- Application submission guidance
- Fee transparency
- Contact information for inspections
- "Like a step-by-step process" that walks providers through everything before they start the application

## Important Contextual Information

### Current Software Usage
1. **KinderSmart** - State attendance tracking (DCYF mandated)
2. **KidCare** - CACFP food program logging
3. **Email** - Document delivery to parents
4. **Manual processes** - Most paperwork, filing, receipt storage

### Regulatory Context
- **State licensor** - Very specific about using KinderSmart for attendance
- **Food program** - Closed system, requires being a sponsor to access, less open to third-party apps
- **Attendance tracking** - State allows third-party apps, but her licensor mandates specific one
- **Brightwheel** - Aware of it (largest daycare software), hasn't tried due to licensor requirements

### Daily/Recurring Tasks
**Daily**:
- Attendance tracking
- Food logging (during nap time)
- Check previous day's sign-outs and complete them

**Weekly**:
- Grocery shopping for food program
- Ongoing paperwork management

**Monthly**:
- Submit food program data to sponsor
- Invoice subsidy kids through DCYF
- Receive authorization letters for subsidy children

**Yearly**:
- Quarterly sponsor check-ins (4x/year)
- Early learning coach meetings (as needed)
- Tax preparation
- Licensing renewals
- Certificate renewals

### Subsidy System
- Parents apply through DCYF (welfare system, same place as food stamps)
- Once approved, parents provide provider number to daycare
- Provider sometimes helps walk parents through application process
- State monitors subsidy kid attendance very closely (paying for care)
- Provider gets letters in the mail about authorizations and changes
- Provider invoices online, receives checks by mail

## Design Implications

### Must-Have Features (Based on Top Priorities)
1. **Digital onboarding workflow** with e-signatures and document management
2. **Receipt scanning and storage** (mobile-friendly)
3. **Document organization system** for licenses, certificates, invoices
4. **Parent communication** with privacy controls
5. **Photo sharing** with permission management

### Important Considerations
- Provider pays for app, parents use for free (her suggested model)
- Must work on both mobile (for receipts, photos) and desktop/tablet (for paperwork)
- Integration or compatibility with state systems (attendance, subsidy invoicing)
- Simple enough for non-tech-savvy parents
- Offline/async workflows (parents and providers on different schedules)

### Regulatory Constraints
- State licensor may mandate specific software for certain functions
- Food program currently closed to third-party development
- Attendance tracking more open but subject to licensor approval
- Privacy and data security for children critical

## Conclusion

Lara's biggest pain points center around **paperwork management** and **onboarding processes**. While technology issues with attendance tracking are frustrating, her top priority would be solutions that:
1. Digitize and streamline the onboarding paperwork process
2. Provide easy receipt scanning and organization for tax purposes
3. Organize ongoing licensing and certification documents
4. Enable better parent communication without privacy concerns

The licensing setup process was difficult but is less relevant to her current daily operations. Attendance tracking is a mandatory pain point due to state requirements and poor software, but she's resigned to the current system since her licensor mandates it.

**Recommendation**: Focus initial product development on paperwork management and digital onboarding, as these provide immediate value and aren't blocked by regulatory mandates requiring specific software.
